About Me

I'm a Ph.D. student passionate about bridging machine learning and optimization to solve complex financial problems. My research focuses on developing innovative approaches that combine deep learning with mathematical optimization for portfolio management and financial decision-making.

Currently pursuing my Ph.D. at UNIST (Ulsan National Institute of Science and Technology), I work in the Financial Engineering Lab where we explore the intersection of AI, optimization theory, and quantitative finance. My work aims to create practical solutions that leverage the power of machine learning while maintaining the mathematical rigor of traditional optimization methods.

I'm particularly interested in decision-focused learning approaches that directly optimize for downstream decision quality, rather than just prediction accuracy. This perspective drives my research in portfolio optimization and financial modeling.
